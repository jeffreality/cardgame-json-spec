Go Math: Official Rules

Go Math is an educational card game where each card shows a simple arithmetic expression. Your goal is to collect cards whose values match the total you’re seeking — think of it like “Go Fish” meets arithmetic!

Objective

Be the player with the most matches by the end of the game. A “match” means two or more cards whose values (e.g., 2, 10) create a valid set in your hand.

Setup

  • Shuffle the entire Go Math deck thoroughly.
  • Deal 5 cards to each player (adjust for different play styles). Place the remainder in the center to form a draw pile.
  • Pick a starting player randomly.

Gameplay

  1. Ask for a total: On your turn, pick a value you need to complete a set in your hand. For example, if you have a card “3+5” (value = 8), you might ask, “Do you have a value of 8?”
  2. Other player checks: The targeted player must pass you any cards in their hand matching that total (e.g., any card with value = 8). If they don’t have any, they say “Go Math!”, and you draw the top card from the center pile.
  3. Continue turns: As long as you collect at least one card from the player asked, you take another turn. Otherwise, play passes to the next player in clockwise order.
  4. Form sets: Whenever you have a pair (or more) of cards that total the same value, lay them down as a completed set. Keep them in front of you.

Example: You have “3+3” and “8-2,” both equal 6. You can lay them down as a completed set worth 8.

Ending the Game

  • Play continues until all cards are drawn from the pile and no more matches can be made.
  • The player who has laid down the most sets wins.

Have fun, and happy learning!
